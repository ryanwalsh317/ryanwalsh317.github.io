DNA "Tricoder": The World's First Mobile Genome Sequence Analyzer

December 9th, 2020.



Cold Springs Harbor Laboratory scientists have developed the world's first mobile genome sequence analyzer, called iGenomics (very creative name). If you pair a phone with the sequencer that the app is designed to work with, you'll get a mobile genetics lab.

The device is being compared to Star Trek's "tricorder" as the device in the show was used to scan, analyze, and record data. This device does a similar thing, as it only needs a small sample of something to scan with the scanner, like a virus sample, and it will map the sequences of whatever was used.

Professor Micheal Schatz oversaw Aspyn Palatnick's development of the app, which took a whole 8 years. Aspyn Palatnick started as an intern at Cold Springs Harbor Laboratory at 14, an astoundingly young age.

Aspyn Palatnick's main reason for creating this was to create a small and portable way to analyze DNA, as he said, "As the sequencers continued to get even smaller, there were no technologies availablel to let you study that DNA on a mobile device. Most of the studying of DNA: aligning, analyzing, is done on lever server-clusters or high-end laptops."

This kind of technology could even be brought to space, as it is easier to isolate a virus and not have to have an incredibly powerful computer to bring with you. You don't even need a connection to the internet to use this device. That is, after you install it on your phone.

The algorithm can apparently quickly map DNA sequences of viral pathogens, such as the flu, Zika, or even Covid-19. They even provide an online tutorial of using the device with Covid-19 to truly show the power of this device. Link is here: http://schatz-lab.org/iGenomics/tutorial/
