Microsoft in heavy talks to buy AI communications firm Nuance for $16 billion

14 April 2021



The Microsoft logo with CEO Satya Nadella.

Microsoft is in advanced talks with Nuance Communications to acquire it for $16 billion via stock options for its advanced AI speech recognition. This decision will expand their business with many medical companies across the globe. The company specializes in transcriptions of things like medical reports, which are especially important in times where "telehealth", or doctor's appointments over the phone, are booming and are likely here to stay.

Nearly 80% of US hospitals are already using Nuance, making the acquisition even more of a no-brainer for Microsoft. Nuance says that if it joins forces with Microsoft, it could deepen its cloud-based offerings, while also tapping in with Microsoft's global customer base. The two companies even formed a sort-of partnership in 2019 that automates clinical administrative work.

The acquisition marks another huge deposit for Microsoft, as this is the second largest acquisition since the $27 billion purchase of LinkedIn. This also helps with Microsoft's recent expansion into speech recognition due to the companies' other specialization into transcriptions from speech to document.

Not much else has been said at the moment, but Microsoft is likely to purchase the company by Monday of next week. Will Microsoft burst forward into a new industry, or will it fail and leave Microsoft with yet another dead company?
